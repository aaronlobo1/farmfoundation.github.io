FAQs

Some of your Questions Answered:

Q. How does the foundation work?

The foundation is created with the belief that effective digital currencies have the potential to give a lot back to the society. The FARM Token foundation ecosystem consists of a non- profit and a for-profit arm. The non-profit arm would work with philanthropy projects, R&D into sustainable farming, conservation of nature and wildlife. The for-profit arm would work and interact with entrepreneurs, partner companies and the FARM Token Community through the forum

Q. Why food?

Food is an essential part of people’s lives, and as such is much more than just a means of survival. It is also the main factor in how we view ourselves and others. It plays a big role in all social and political issues, and is a bastion of popular media. As the growth of middle class in developing countries surges we anticipate a change in consumer taste and a huge demand for quality food. Additionally, with many states in the USA warming up to the idea of legalizing marijuana we believe that the food sector is poised for rapid growth especially with cannabis edibles/beverages..

Q. How do I participate with the foundation?

The only way to participate with the foundation is by supporting the FARM Token financially

Q. Is this like Shark Tank for food??

In a way yes. On Shark Tank we found that food companies outperformed their counterparts and that success was one of the key influencer in creating this foundation.

Q. Who will be the directors managing the funds of the foundation?

The idea that being smart in one domain qualifies one to just dabble in another is dangerous. Hence we intend to appoint directors who are seasoned venture capitalists to manage the funds.

Q. What is the problem that FARM token is solving?

Supply chain transparency, Food recall, food fraud. Not only can traceability facilitated through blockchain improve food safety; it can also help to reduce fraud in the industry When an apple or lamb chop finally makes it to your dinner table, you will have a way of verifying its origins or packaging claims such as “organic” and “non-GMO. Kosher or halal”
